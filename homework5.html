Comparatives & Superlatives · as + adjective + as · not as + adjective + as

-er / more the -est / the most than as ... as not as ... as

1) Complete the text

Use the comparatives and superlatives to complete the text according to the context. Remember that the and than are part of the structure.

1(old) + (beautiful)
My name is Gabrielle. I live in (old) and (beautiful) city in Colombia.
2(exuberant)
It is called Santa Marta. It is located nearby (exuberant) sea: the Caribbean Sea.
3(important) + (large)
The tourist areas the place offers are considered (important) ones mostly because it has (large) number of hotels.
4(more) + (interesting)
It has also become one of (more) visited areas having also (interesting) variety of restaurants.
5(tall) + (high)
Sierra Nevada is (tall) coastal mountain region in the world. It has (high) views than Cali and other beautiful places in Colombia.
6(cheap) + (comfortable)
If you go to this wonderful landscape you don't spend too much money because you can go camping: it is (cheap) staying in a hotel. When you camp you can even sleep (comfortable) sleeping in a hotel.
7(pure) + (good)
In here you have plenty of space and (pure) air. In this place you have everything you need to spend (good) time with your family.
Tip: Superlative: the + -est / the most. Comparative: -er / more + than. Equality: as ... as.

2) As ... as / Not as ... as

Write sentences using as + adjective + as or not as + adjective + as.

1as ... as
That tree is big. Our house is equally big.
2not as ... as
My bag is heavier than Paul’s bag.
3not as ... as
Documentaries are more important than fiction movies.
4(heavy)
Marla is 65 kilos. Marian is 65 kilos too.
5(young)
Margo is 13 years old and Mariela is 17.
6as ... as
Rio Claro is hot. Ciudad Neily is equally hot.
7not as ... as
Ciudad Neily is hot. San Vito is not so hot.

3) Complete the chart

Use the comparative, superlative or as + adjective to complete the chart appropriately.

1(tall)
My sister is so much than me.
2(large)
My old house was much than my new one.
3(nice)
She is the person in the class.
4(interesting)
Maths is by far the subject at school.
5(kind)
That was the thing anyone ever did for me.
6(expensive)
That store is than the one near my house.
7(good)
My marks at school aren’t as as yours.
8(sad)
That was the film I’ve ever seen.
9(boring)
This book is as as the last one I read.
10(bad)
Those were the holidays of my life.
Remember: Irregulars: good → better → the best, bad → worse → the worst.
